## Design Context

### Users

People reading long-form PDFs on desktop, laptop, or touch devices who want the focus and physical familiarity of a real book without uploading their document.

### Brand Personality

Tactile, calm, and editorial. Folio should feel crafted and private rather than technical, glossy, or game-like.

### Aesthetic Direction

An organic, refined reading room: uncoated cream paper, woven cloth covers, restrained green chrome, natural shadows, and motion that behaves like lightweight paper. The core experience should remain light and quiet enough for sustained reading.

### Design Principles

1. Preserve the physical-book metaphor in material, motion, and sound.
2. Keep input immediate and page movement continuous, interruptible, and low-latency.
3. Use multimodal feedback sparingly: rustle during a turn, soft contact on landing.
4. Keep PDFs and generated feedback local to the browser.
5. Maintain 60fps with compositor-friendly effects and respect reduced-motion preferences.
